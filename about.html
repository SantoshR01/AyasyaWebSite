About

Built around the parts of software work everyone underrates.

Most of a system's cost is paid after launch. We built Ayasya around that fact.

Why Ayasya exists

Ayasya Technologies started from a simple frustration: most engagements optimize for the launch, then disappear. The product ships, the deck looks great, and six months later nobody on the client's side knows why the invoicing job silently stopped running.

We work the other way around. Every engagement — a new product, a maintenance contract, an AI feature — is scoped with the assumption that someone will need to understand and change this code a year from now, possibly not us.

How we think about AI

We integrate AI into existing products when it solves a specific, named problem — not as a headline feature. That usually means it's smaller and less flashy than what gets pitched in a sales deck, and it usually means it actually gets used.

If AI isn't the right tool for what you're describing, we'll say so before we say yes to the project.

What we hold ourselves to
Scope

Written down, agreed on, referred back to

Engagements start with a scoping document both sides sign off on — so "quick addition" conversations happen on purpose, not by accident.

Handover

Maintenance

We answer after the invoice is paid

Support contracts include real response-time commitments, not a best-effort inbox that goes quiet.
